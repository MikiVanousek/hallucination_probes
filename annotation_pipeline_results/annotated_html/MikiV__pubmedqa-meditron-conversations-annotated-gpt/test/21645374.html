context: Programmed cell death (PCD) is the regulated death of cells within an organism. The lace plant (Aponogeton madagascariensis) produces perforations in its leaves through PCD. The leaves of the plant consist of a latticework of longitudinal and transverse veins enclosing areoles. PCD occurs in the cells at the center of these areoles and progresses outwards, stopping approximately five cells from the vasculature. The role of mitochondria during PCD has been recognized in animals; however, it has been less studied during PCD in plants. The following paper elucidates the role of mitochondrial dynamics during developmentally regulated PCD in vivo in A. madagascariensis. A single areole within a window stage leaf (PCD is occurring) was divided into three areas based on the progression of PCD; cells that will not undergo PCD (NPCD), cells in early stages of PCD (EPCD), and cells in late stages of PCD (LPCD). user_prompt: Do mitochondria play a role in remodelling lace plant leaves during programmed cell death?
yes Explanation: The results show that mitochondria undergo a series of dynamic changes during PCD in lace plant leaves. The mitochondrial membrane potential (ΔΨm) is maintained in the NPCD region, but decreases in EPCD and LPCD. These results suggest that mitochondrial dynamics and ΔΨm are involved in the regulation of PCD in lace plant leaves. The results also suggest that PTP formation during PCD is a potential mechanism by which the mitochondrial membrane potential is disrupted.
